### **[Understanding Metaphors in Design](<Understanding Metaphors in Design.md>)**

#### **Definition**:

- Metaphors are rhetorical tools that represent one concept using another similar concept, enriching mental imagery and aiding conceptual understanding.
    

#### **Key Concepts of Metaphors**:

1. **Simplifying Complex Ideas**:
    
    - Break down intricate concepts into relatable, everyday terms.
        
    - Example: Computer memory described as a "filing cabinet" makes it easier to understand data storage.
        
2. **Creating Emotional Connections**:
    
    - Metaphors evoke emotional responses, helping brands resonate with audiences.
        
    - Example: Apple’s “Think Different” inspires individuality and creativity.
        
3. **Enhancing Recall**:
    
    - Memorable metaphors make designs and ideas distinctive.
        
    - Example: Velcro’s hook-and-loop mechanism inspired by burrs is both functional and memorable.
        
4. **Applications in Design**:
    
    - **Biomimicry**: Engineering solutions inspired by nature, such as bird-like aircraft wings.
        
    - **Digital Systems**: Representing abstract digital processes with tangible metaphors like "cloud storage."

### **[Prototyping in Engineering Design](<Prototyping in Engineering Design.md>)**

#### **Definition**:

- Prototyping involves creating tangible or digital models to test and validate ideas before production.
    

#### **Key Aspects of Prototyping**:

1. **Importance**:
    
    - Enables early detection of design flaws.
        
    - Facilitates iterative improvements based on real-world testing.
        
2. **Rapid Prototyping**:
    
    - Utilizes additive manufacturing (e.g., 3D printing) for quick model creation.
        
    - Converts CAD designs into physical or digital prototypes layer by layer.
        
3. **Types of Processes**:
    
    - **Subtractive**: Removes material (e.g., milling, grinding).
        
    - **Compressive**: Shapes materials through molding or casting.
        
4. **Applications**:
    
    - Used in fields like product design, engineering, and software development to refine concepts efficiently.

### **[Concept Generation in Design](<Concept Generation in Design.md>)**

#### **Definition**:

- Concept generation involves creating innovative ideas through structured techniques like metaphors, blending, and thematic approaches.
    

#### **Methods of Concept Generation**:

1. **Metaphor Method**:
    
    - Uses analogies to generate new design concepts.
        
    - Example: The Swan Chair evokes elegance by mimicking the shape of a swan.
        
2. **Blend Method**:
    
    - Combines distinct concepts to create innovative solutions.
        
    - Example: Solar-powered electric vehicles merge renewable energy with transportation.
        
3. **Thematic Method**:
    
    - Explores ideas centered around a unifying theme.
        
    - Example: Sustainability themes in packaging and architecture inspire eco-friendly innovations.

### **[Sketching and Visualization](<Sketching and Visualization.md>)**

#### **Definition**:

- Sketching is a rapid, freehand drawing method used to express and refine design ideas visually.
    

#### **Key Aspects**:

1. **Purpose**:
    
    - Serves as an early prototype to evaluate and iterate concepts.
        
    - Encourages collaborative discussions around design ideas.
        
2. **K-Scripts**:
    
    - Text-based frameworks outlining user interactions with products.
        
    - Highlight observable actions and underlying motivations.
        
3. **Dynamic Visuals**:
    
    - Enhance understanding through quick, iterative sketches or diagrams.
