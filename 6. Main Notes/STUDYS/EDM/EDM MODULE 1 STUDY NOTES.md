# **[Engineering Design and Modelling](Engineering%20Design%20and%20Modelling.md)**

### **What is Design?**

- A creative planning process to develop new products and systems.
- Driven by **criteria** (desired features/functions) and **constraints** (limitations like size, cost).

### **Engineering Design**

- Purposeful method for practical problem-solving.
- Iterative process influenced by criteria and constraints.

### **The Four C’s of Design**:

1. **Creativity**: Creating novel ideas or concepts.
2. **Complexity**: Decision-making across numerous variables.
3. **Compromise**: Balancing conflicting requirements.
4. **Choice**: Selecting among alternatives.

# **[Dieter Rams' Ten Principles of Good Design](Dieter%20Rams'%20Ten%20Principles%20of%20Good%20Design.md)**

1. Innovative
2. Useful
3. Aesthetic
4. Understandable
5. Unobtrusive
6. Honest
7. Long-lasting
8. Thorough (down to the last detail)
9. Environmentally friendly
10. Minimal

---

# **[Types of Design](Types%20of%20Design.md)**

1. **Original Design**: Innovative, often disruptive (e.g., microprocessor).
2. **Adaptive Design**: Modifies existing solutions for new needs.
3. **Redesign**: Improves cost, function, or reliability of existing designs.
4. **Selection Design**: Choosing components (e.g., motors, pumps).
5. **Industrial Design**: Focuses on aesthetic and sensory appeal.

---

# **[Human-Centered Design (HCD)](Human-Centered%20Design%20(HCD).md)**

**Phases**:

1. **Observation**: Understand user behavior and pain points.
2. **Ideation**: Brainstorming solutions.
3. **Rapid Prototyping**: Create low-fidelity prototypes.
4. **User Feedback**: Test and gather insights.
5. **Iteration**: Refine designs based on feedback.
6. **Implementation**: Finalize and launch.

---

# **Design Thinking**

- **Key Principles**:
    - Human-centric innovation.
    - Combine divergent and convergent thinking.
    - Test early, fail fast, iterate.
- **Process**:
    - Define the problem.
    - Identify needs (obvious and hidden).
    - Prototype and test.

---

# **[Tools for Design Thinking](Tools%20for%20Design%20Thinking.md)**

1. **Stakeholder Map**: Identifies all impacted groups.
2. **Empathy Map**: Understands user thoughts, feelings, and actions.
3. **5-Whys**: Root cause analysis.
4. **AEIOU Method**: Activities, Environment, Interactions, Objects, Users.
5. **Persona Method**: Create user archetypes.
6. **Storytelling**: Present ideas via narratives.
7. **Journey Mapping**: Visualize user interactions.

# **[Enhancing Creativity](Enhancing%20Creativity.md)**
