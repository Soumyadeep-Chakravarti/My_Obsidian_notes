### **Types of Design**

1. **Conceptual Design**:
    
    - Initial stage to generate and evaluate design concepts.
2. **Embodiment Design**:
    
    - Converts concepts into physical forms.
    - **Key Activities**:
        - **Product Architecture**: Defines modules and interfaces.
        - **Configuration Design**: Specifies shape, dimensions, and assembly.
        - **Parametric Design**: Determines design variables like dimensions and tolerances.

---

### **Embodiment Design Details**

1. **Product Architecture**:
    
    - Organizes physical elements into modules (e.g., subsystems).
    - Styles:
        - **Modular**: Well-defined modules, shorter development cycle.
        - **Integral**: Components serve multiple functions, reducing part count.
2. **Types of Modular Architecture**:
    
    - **Slot Modular**: Different interfaces (e.g., car radio vs. DVD player).
    - **Bus Modular**: Common interface (e.g., power bus).
    - **Sectional Modular**: Identical interfaces (e.g., piping systems).

---

### **Configuration Design**

- Establishes shapes, dimensions, and spatial constraints.
- Considers materials, production techniques, and part elimination or combination.

### **Parametric Design**

- Focuses on setting values for design variables to optimize performance and manufacturability.
- **Steps**:
    1. Formulate the problem.
    2. Generate alternative designs.
    3. Analyze alternatives.
    4. Evaluate results.
    5. Refine and optimize.

### **Design Features**

#### **Design for Aesthetics**:

- Focus on sensory appeal:
    - **Vision**: Color, shape, texture.
    - **Hearing**: Loudness, pitch.
    - **Touch**: Texture, comfort.
    - **Taste and Smell**: Especially relevant for food and environmental design.

#### **Design for Production**:

1. Reduce production costs and time.
2. Optimize material selection.
3. Use standard components.

#### **Design for Quality**:

- Types:
    - Performance, reliability, durability, aesthetics, etc.
- Benefits:
    - Cost reduction, customer satisfaction, efficiency.

#### **Design for Usability and User Experience (UX)**:

- Focus on functionality, ease of use, and positive user emotions.

#### **Design for Maintenance**:

- Simplify servicing and inspections.
- Incorporate ergonomic principles for ease of access.

#### **Design for Minimum Cost**:

- Rules:
    - Minimize part count and production complexity.
    - Optimize batch sizes for economies of scale.
