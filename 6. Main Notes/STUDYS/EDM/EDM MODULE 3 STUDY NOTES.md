## **[Sustainable Design](<Sustainable Design.md>)**

### **Key Concepts**

1. **Definition**:
    - Seeks to `reduce negative environmental impacts` through thoughtful design.
    - Focuses on `long-term solutions` for products or systems.
2. **Three Pillars**:
    
    - **Social Sustainability**: Addresses equality, human rights, and social inclusion.
    - **Economic Sustainability**: Ensures affordable, efficient, and equitable solutions.
    - **Environmental Sustainability**: Minimizes ecological harm and resource depletion.
3. **Digital Sustainability**:
    
    - Optimize energy usage in digital products.
    - Example: Dark mode in apps reduces energy consumption.

---

### **[Sustainable Design](<Sustainable Design.md>)**

1. **Definition**:
    - Development that meets current needs without compromising future generations.
2. **Goals**:
    - Minimize environmental harm.
    - Promote renewable energy and efficient resource use.
3. **Examples**:
    - Wind and solar energy.
    - Crop rotation.
    - Sustainable construction.

---

## **[Environmental Crisis](<Environmental Crisis.md>)**

1. **Definition**:
    - When the environment fails to sustain life due to overuse or misuse of resources.
2. **Causes**:
    - Population explosion.
    - Industrialization and urbanization.
    - Deforestation and pollution.

## **[UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)](<UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).md>)**

- Focus on 17 global goals addressing poverty, health, education, equality, and environmental preservation.
- **The Five P's**:
    1. **People**: Social inclusion and equality.
    2. **Planet**: Climate action and biodiversity conservation.
    3. **Prosperity**: Sustainable economic growth.
    4. **Peace**: Inclusive societies and justice for all.
    5. **Partnerships**: Global collaboration for resource mobilization.

### **[Role of Design in Sustainability](<Role of Design in Sustainability.md>)**

1. **Designer’s Responsibility**:
    - Solve problems with innovative, sustainable solutions.
    - Example: Apple reduced energy use in iMacs by 97% since 1998.
2. **Principles of Sustainable Design**:
    - **Form**: Reduce energy consumption, transport, and packaging costs.
    - **Function**: Improve usability, reduce waste.
    - **Cost-Effectiveness**: Lower costs for sustainable products.
    - **Renewable Energy**: Use solar or wind energy.
    - **Durability and Recycling**: Design for longevity and reuse.

---

### **[Design for Environment (DfE)](<Design for Environment (DfE).md>)**

1. **Focus Areas**:
    - Material selection.
    - Production and packaging processes.
    - End-of-life recycling or disposal.
2. **Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)**:
    - Analyzes environmental impacts across all product stages:
        - Raw material extraction → Manufacturing → Use → Disposal.

---

### **[Biomimicry](Biomimicry.md)**

1. **Definition**:
    - Mimics nature's patterns and systems for innovative solutions.
2. **Examples**:
    - **Honeycombs**: Inspired strong, lightweight materials in aerospace.
    - **Velcro**: Based on plant burrs.
    - **Lotus Leaf**: Self-cleaning surfaces.

---

### **[Social Innovation](<Social Innovation.md>)**

- Develop solutions for systemic social and environmental challenges.
- **Examples**:
    - Fair trade practices.
    - Emissions trading programs.
- **Drivers**:
    - Exchange of ideas and values.
    - Integration of private, public, and philanthropic resources.
