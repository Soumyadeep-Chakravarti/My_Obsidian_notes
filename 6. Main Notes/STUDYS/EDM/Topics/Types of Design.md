### **Types of Design**

Engineering design can be categorized into various types based on its purpose, approach, and application. These classifications help define the scope and methodology required for different design projects.

---

### **1. Original Design / Innovative Design**

- **Definition**: A design created from entirely new concepts, often resulting in groundbreaking or disruptive innovations.
- **Characteristics**:
    - Employs original ideas to address a need.
    - Rarely involves inventing the need itself, but occasionally it does.
    - Often introduces new technologies or methods.
- **Examples**:
    - The invention of the microprocessor, which revolutionized computing.
    - The development of the electric car.

---

### **2. Adaptive Design**

- **Definition**: Modifies or adapts existing designs to serve a new purpose or context.
- **Characteristics**:
    - Involves synthesis and slight reconfiguration of known solutions.
    - Typically retains the fundamental principles of the original design.
- **Examples**:
    - Adapting inkjet printing technology for 3D printers.
    - Using drone technology for agricultural crop monitoring.

---

### **3. Redesign**

- **Definition**: Improves or modifies an existing design to enhance performance, reduce cost, or address failures.
- **Characteristics**:
    - May involve changes to materials, shape, or construction methods.
    - Does not alter the basic working principle or concept.
- **Types**:
    - **Failure-Oriented Redesign**: Fixing components prone to failure.
    - **Cost-Oriented Redesign**: Optimizing manufacturing or material costs.
- **Examples**:
    - Replacing a metal component with a lighter composite material.
    - Modifying a car design to meet new safety standards.

---

### **4. Selection Design**

- **Definition**: Focuses on choosing standard components to integrate into a system.
- **Characteristics**:
    - Relies on cataloged components like motors, pumps, or bearings.
    - Balances performance, cost, and quality in the selection process.
- **Examples**:
    - Selecting the most efficient pump for a water treatment system.
    - Choosing a battery type for a smartphone.

---

### **5. Industrial Design**

- **Definition**: Enhances the aesthetic and sensory appeal of a product, often focusing on user interaction.
- **Characteristics**:
    - Blends functionality with artistic elements.
    - Improves the user experience by optimizing ergonomics and usability.
- **Examples**:
    - The sleek design of modern appliances like toasters or coffee makers.
    - The ergonomic layout of gaming controllers.

---

### **Comparison of Design Types**

|**Design Type**|**Purpose**|**Approach**|**Examples**|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Original Design**|Create something entirely new|Inventive and disruptive|Microprocessor, Electric car|
|**Adaptive Design**|Apply existing solutions to new problems|Modifies and synthesizes known ideas|3D printers, Agricultural drones|
|**Redesign**|Improve or fix existing designs|Optimizes materials, costs, or performance|Lightweight car frames|
|**Selection Design**|Choose components for integration|Catalog-based component selection|Smartphone batteries, Water pumps|
|**Industrial Design**|Focus on aesthetics and usability|Combines engineering with artistic input|Gaming consoles, Kitchen tools|

---

### **Summary**

The types of design reflect the diverse objectives and methods in engineering projects. Whether creating entirely new solutions, adapting existing ones, or focusing on aesthetics, each design type plays a unique role in addressing user needs and advancing technology.
