### **[Materials and Modelling](<Materials and Modelling.md>)**

#### **Classification of Engineering Materials**:

1. **Categories**:
    
    - **Metals and Alloys**: Ferrous and non-ferrous metals.
    - **Ceramic Materials**
    - **Organic Polymers**
    - **Composites**
    - **Semiconductors**
2. **Key Points on Metals**:
    
    - Polycrystalline structures.
    - Alloys enhance strength and required properties.
    - Ferrous metals: e.g., pig iron, cast iron, wrought iron, steels.
    - Non-ferrous metals: e.g., copper, aluminum, brass.

#### **Main Types of Iron**:

1. **Pig Iron**: High carbon content, basic material for cast iron and steel.
2. **Cast Iron**:
    - Composition: 1.7%-6.67% carbon, with small amounts of silicon, manganese, etc.
    - Properties: High compressive strength, low tensile strength, brittle.
    - Applications: Machine parts, pipes, manhole covers, engine components.
3. **Wrought Iron**: Malleable, low carbon content.
4. **Steel**:
    - Alloy of iron and carbon (up to 1.7% carbon).
    - Types:
        - Plain Carbon Steels: Dead, low, medium, high carbon steels.
        - Alloy Steels: Includes stainless steel, tool steel.

### **[Stress and Strain](Stress%20and%20Strain.md)**

#### **Stress**:

- Definition: Internal resisting force per unit area.
- Types:
    - Normal (Axial), Tension, Compression, Shear, Torsion, Thermal.

#### **Strain**:

- Ratio of deformation to the original dimension.
- Types: Longitudinal, Lateral.

#### **Elasticity and Elastic Limit**:

- Elasticity: Ability of material to regain original shape post-deformation.
- Elastic Limit: Maximum stress before permanent deformation.

#### **Hooke’s Law**:

- Within elastic limit, stress ∝ strain.
- Modulus of Elasticity: Ratio of stress to strain.

### **[Mechanical Properties of Materials](Mechanical%20Properties%20of%20Materials.md)**

1. **Strength**: Resistance to external loading.
2. **Elasticity**: Recovery to original shape after stress removal.
3. **Plasticity**: Permanent deformation without failure.
4. **Ductility**: Drawn into thin wires (e.g., copper).
5. **Malleability**: Flattened into sheets (e.g., aluminum).
6. **Brittleness**: Breaks without deformation (e.g., glass).
7. **Toughness**: Absorbs energy before failure (e.g., steel).
8. **Hardness**: Resistance to scratching and indentation.
9. **Resilience**: Absorbs energy elastically.
