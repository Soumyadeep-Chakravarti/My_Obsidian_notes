People say, “Ah, to be young again,  
to fall madly in love anew,  
to feel invincible once more—  
as if we could turn back time  
to those days now lost in memory,  
when laughter was our melody,  
and every heartbeat felt like destiny.”

They dream of endless summer skies,  
of evenings kissed by fireflies,  
where promises were whispered soft,  
and dreams took flight, aloft.

Yet time marches on, relentless,  
its lessons etched with scars.  
But what holds us back from love,  
from feeling our best,  
from being unstoppable?

Is it fear that shadows us,  
or the rigidity of age?  
Or merely time’s relentless bite?

I can’t say for sure, for I am still young,  
with a world to explore,  
to fall deeply in love with,  
to strive to be my best,  
to be unstoppable.  
And if I fall short of that,  
can I truly say I did not live?

I will have loved,  
seen the world to my heart’s content.  
And if not unstoppable,  
I will have left a mark—  
somewhere, on someone, on something, or at least in time.

Will that be enough?  
Enough to satisfy the spirit in me?  
Only time can tell.
